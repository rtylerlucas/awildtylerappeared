A New Year is Here

Welcome to my freshly minted website. This post covers highlights from the year past and personal goals for the new year.

Highlights of 2019

2019 - The Best We Could Do

Last year I wrote a personal review of the year prior, with goals set going into 2019.

My 2019 goals were quite humble. (Play piano, meditate, write, etc)1[^¹]. If I were to pick a theme for last year's goals, it would be sustainability. I was coming off a strong 2018. I had just started a new job @ Convoy. My goals were focused on building healthy habits that could last a lifetime. Some were achieved, some in partial capacity, and some not at all.

2020's goals are more incremental. For starters, they are not annual, but monthly.

Future goals are targeted more toward outcomes and less toward routines. This is not to discredit the power of habit, far from it. I'm a huge believer in (Queue => Routine => Reward). Yet I've found, at least personally, setting goals against habits can decrement the magnitude of their benefits. This is partially due to my myopic tendency to shift toward checking boxes rather than focusing on why I chose that goal in the first place. I do not need an annual goal of brushing my teeth 730 times a year, for example. I brush my teeth to maintain a healthy smile. If set a goal of 730 brushes, perhaps I knock all 730 out in Q1; my teeth proceed to deteriorate the rest of the year. Or maybe the opposite - Q3 rolls around and I only brushed a couple dozen times - I'll be hard pressed to even attempt reaching 730, knowing how far I am from the target. Setting a goal too granular, (E.g “brush teeth twice a day, everyday”) also work for me, for if I miss a month I'm unlikely to pick it back up again knowing I failed on the ‘everyday’ part of the puzzle.

The goal should be the raison d'etre, not the means. Lofty goals, simple steps.

Goals

Goals in January 2020

OK - So I decided to revamp my goal-setting. How does it look? Heavily inspired by John Doerr's OKR System, I set a goal for the month, (The “Objective”), then list a couple steps that I think will get me to that goal (The “Key Results”).3

Goal: Build a healthy website. I see the personal website as a gateway for improvement in several avenues of life. Creative, social, professional².

(1) Launch a Personal Timeline feature on this site

I love the idea of a succinct timeline view of major events in someone's life. It's the story we tell ourselves, through whatever lense fits. LinkedIn is one example, is a timeline scoped to your professional career. Here is more ‘personal life timeline’ example that I enjoy.

(2) Make a ‘Top Ten’ post

‘Top Ten’ is a simple pattern that transforms a topic into something easy to read, write, and talk about. I'm setting the bar quite low here - all the more reason to #GetShitDone.4 Some potential topics of mine:

  • Documentaries
  • Vegan Meals
  • Movies involving Time Travel
  • Celebrity Role Models

(3) Make a post about the Electrical Grid, Survivorship, and Self Sustainability

I am nearly done with ‘The Grid’, by Gretchen Bakke.
